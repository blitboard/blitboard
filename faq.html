Frequently Asked Questions

Got a question? We've got answers. If you have some other questions, contact us directly.

Is my code still mine after I submit over here?

Absolutely! We work with your code being called yours until you Github account is yours. Since the piece you are asked to share is a part of a you Github repository, the piece will always its maintain its identity under your accounts.

Why cannot I see my code on the page anymore?

Currently, we work for the better code. We praise people with shorter code. These people are Gods of this planet. Make sure the artwork you worked on has no, or just useful comments, smaller variable, but is still a lot easier to understand. Last but not the least, it should be working.

Is my privacy concerned on Blitboard?

Sharing your identity is totally your call. Its just the matter of fact that your responses towards a page we have on the web should have a bond with you and therefore you are asked to publicise yourself in a way you want.

What all can I embed along with my code?

You can come up with anything you want. It just should be embed-able. Be it Youtube video or maybe a Tweet. Make sure your posts are not private and follow all basic Social Media guidelines and presentable for all active domains on a positive note.

What after I have mailed my code?

After we have recieved your mail, we will see if the code perfectly balances out with our conditions and then you will recieve a shareable link for your code which you can publish as an article on LinkedIn or post it on Facebook or, eat it with some soy sauce.

Where can I find Blitboard on social platforms?

Unfortunately, we are not yet part of the Social Planet, but soon we will be having a LinkedIn and a Facebook page with some exciting things coming up. You can contact us on teamblitboard@gmail.com
